Responsible Gaming — Time, Money & Wellbeing Guide

How to protect your time, money and mental health while using apps like Agni Club — with warning signs and international helplines.

Table of contents

  3. Warning signs of problem gambling

The right mindset

Every color-prediction round is a random event. The most experienced player and the newest player have the same objective odds on any single round. Everything else — 'strategies', 'patterns', 'systems' — is either coincidence or storytelling. Internalising this one fact protects you from most bad decisions.

Treat any money you deposit as spent the moment it leaves your bank account. If that framing feels uncomfortable, you should not be depositing that amount. This is not a punishing rule — it is the same principle you already apply to a cinema ticket or a restaurant meal.

Setting your limits

Before you play, decide two numbers: the maximum time you will spend today, and the maximum money you will risk this month. Write them down. Use a timer for time and an app-level or bank-level cap for money. When either limit is reached, stop for the day — no exceptions, no 'one more round'. The moment you allow one exception, the limit stops working.

Warning signs of problem gambling

  • You are playing longer than you planned, most days.
  • You have deposited more than you can comfortably afford.
  • You are chasing losses with increasingly large stakes.
  • You are hiding your play from family or partner.
  • You feel restless, anxious or irritable when not playing.
  • You have borrowed money to play, or used money meant for essentials.

Any two of these together is a serious warning. Please pause and talk to a helpline — the numbers below are free and confidential.

Self-exclusion tools

Most apps offer a self-exclusion option — a way to voluntarily block yourself from the service for a fixed period. Use it without guilt. On the device side, screen-time limits (Digital Wellbeing on Android, Screen Time on iOS) let you cap daily minutes on the app. Your bank may also offer a 'gambling block' that prevents transactions with gambling merchants; check with your bank support to enable it.

Helplines by country

  • India — iCall: 9152987821 (Mon–Sat 8am–10pm); Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 (24×7).
  • United Kingdom — GamCare National Gambling Helpline: 0808 8020 133 (24×7).
  • United States — National Problem Gambling Helpline: 1-800-GAMBLER (24×7).
  • Australia — Gambling Help Online: 1800 858 858 (24×7).
  • Singapore — National Council on Problem Gambling: 1800-6-668-668.

All of these services are free, confidential, and staffed by people trained specifically for this.

If you are worried about someone else

Approaching a family member or friend is delicate. Lead with concern, not accusation. Do not lecture, do not shame, and do not manage their money for them uninvited. Share a helpline number and offer to sit with them while they call it. The most useful role you can play is patient support, not enforcement.

Recovery is possible

Problem gambling is a well-studied condition and it is treatable. Cognitive behavioural therapy, peer support groups like Gamblers Anonymous, and structured self-exclusion have strong evidence bases. Reaching out is the hardest step; everything after it is easier than staying stuck.
